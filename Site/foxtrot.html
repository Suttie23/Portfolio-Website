Our Story

Back in 2019, myself and close friend Michael Surgeon decided to act upon a childhood dream - starting a company in the tech industry. Our idea was simple, create bespoke PC's for clients.

At time time, we were only 18 years of age, so the thought of opening a company was frightening yet exciting - nevertheless, we got two more like minded friends; Douglas Greig and Liam Chisholm and the FoxTrot team was founded.

In our year of operation, we achieved a lot for a young and inexperienced team - but ultimately, our timing could not have been worse as the COVID-19 pandemic was looming over us and would eventually lead to the company being made dormant due to the challanges we, and the world, faced. This page is dedicated to my time as COO of FoxTrot computers and how it has helped me become a better, more skilled person.

Macmillan 24 Hour Charity Livestream

Each of us at FoxTrot have had our lives impacted by the awful disease that is cancer. Now that we were in a position to do so, we decided to participate in the 2019 MacMillan 24 hour live stream against cancer in December using FoxTrot as our platform to raise money to fight the disease. We set our goal initially as £250 - however, by the end of the 24 hour stream we had doubled our target and reached £501. At this point, FoxTrot had only been operating for a month, so this was a massive achievement and one that I remain very proud of to this day.
