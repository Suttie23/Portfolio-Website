Webflix

The WebFlix Application was my HND Year 2 graded unit and involved creating a streaming site that included; A login / registration system, PayPal Sandbox Support, a backend Admin CMS and a supporting backend database.

The project was created using HTML5, PhP, CSS, JS and a MySQL database hooked up to the backend via PhPMyadmin. This database held all of the movie and TV show data that would populate the website. In addition to this, the database held user data (username, password, subscription status, etc) to allow users to login after signing up.

Bright Purple Mock

As part of my Foundation Apprenticeship work placement, I was placed with the recruitment company Bright Purple for a year and tasked with redesigning a new website. This involved looking up the competitors of Bright Purple in order to see what they had done to bring in customers and led to me designing a questionnaire that was presented to all office staff. This questionnaire included questions to figure out what employees liked and disliked about their current website as well as that of their competitors and served as a way to gather the offices' thoughts on what should be included in their new website.

This questionnaire was then turned into the brief and scope of the website I would be developing. Initially, this design was to be followed to create a new site using HTML, PhP, and JS to design the front end as well as a MySQL database to handle data-driven elements of the website. However, due to time restrictions related to the nature of the Foundation Apprenticeship (the website not being my only focus) and the scale of the project it was decided to adopt a Parallax homepage template of which I would then develop the rest of the website around as previously planned.

The website was finished on time and was presented using a localhost server to the company's directors by myself and was well received, however, due to issues regarding communication between the college and work placement as well as the nature of the placement, the website prototype was never taken further.

Grabvans

Grab vans was my first real involvement in any sort of game design, as well as my first time using Mono-game within C#.
The idea for grab vans came around as part of a group brainstorming session for a project that we could all work on as a team as both a means of learning the basics of game development as well as delving deeper into the more complicated work involved in programming a game as part of a team.

The game could have gone in multiple different directions before we had even started writing the code for it - during the design phase we had multiple ideas ranging from a 2D platformer to a 2D brawler. Both of these ideas would have involved a much steeper design and development curve but they both had something in common; pickups.
It was decided that the game should involve some sort of pickup system, and the more we thought about it, the more we were inclined to base the game solely on pickups, and thus, the pitch for Grab Vans was born - A capture the flag style game that revolved around a randomly spawning parcel that must be captured by the player(s) in order to score points for their respective deliver depots while avoiding the deadly traps on the road that could very well bring an end to your delivery driver days!

As well as avoiding dangers on the road, the players had to avoid each other. A single tap from an opposing van deals damage to you and opens your vans doors - dropping the parcel and allowing your foe to pick it up!
The game was presented at an expo at Edinburgh College Granton Campus where visitors were able to play. The game was well recieved and played by most in attendance!

Unfortunately, the code has since been lost to me - however, I'm sure someone from the course will still have a copy that I aim to find. Despite this, I was able to learn a lot from developing this little game and it created the basis of teamwork that I'd use for all projects going forward.

Pod Eachdraidh

The Pod-Eachdraidh team was formed in November of 2021 and consisted of four sound design students, and a software engineering student. In January, the team had their first meeting with the clients. The project was discussed, and the project ambitions and ideas were put forward.

The aim of the project was to turn the physical war diary of Malvern resident "Lorna Lloyd" into a podcast that was easy to access - in addition to brining the diary to light, the clients would be using the podcast and its impact on those that listened to them as well as using us as a case study to see how handling the diaries impacted out impression of the time period.

The team consisted of two parts and was a multi-disciplinary team. The sound design team (consisting of 4 members) and the web development team which consisted of myself. While I was able to provide input to the sound design given I have a brief background in sound, I left the practical aspect to them given their expertise.

I was responsible to creating an RSS script to distribute the podcast once it was complete, this feed was used for a lenghty period of time, however, the clients later expressed interest in having a more flexible method that didn't require any work from a programmer, so I pitched RSS.com as a hosting service / RSS generator which was later adopted in place of the original script.

Additionally, I was working alongside the Malvern Museum web team at Nepeta in order to develop the required pages for their existing wordpress site. The design and creation of the Lorna Lloyd pages fell to me, which can be found in the link below as well as the podcasts should anyone be interested!

Our project was shortlisted out of hundreds of others into a group of 5 to present at Computershare Edinburgh in front of their team and the other groups - details of which can be found below!
